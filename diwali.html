Diwali – Festival of Lights

Diwali is one of the most important and widely celebrated festivals in India. It is known as the Festival of Lights and is celebrated by millions of people across the country. The festival usually falls in October or November every year. People decorate their homes with diyas, candles, lanterns, and colorful lights to make their houses bright and beautiful. Streets, markets, and temples are also decorated with lights and decorations. Diwali represents happiness, positivity, and the victory of light over darkness.

The festival of Diwali has great religious and cultural significance in India. According to Hindu mythology, it celebrates the return of Lord Rama to Ayodhya after defeating the demon king Ravana and completing fourteen years of exile. To welcome him, the people of Ayodhya lit rows of oil lamps everywhere. Because of this tradition, the festival is also called Deepavali, which means a row of lights. This story teaches people that truth and goodness always win over evil.

During Diwali, people clean and decorate their homes to welcome Goddess Lakshmi, the goddess of wealth and prosperity. Beautiful rangoli designs are made at the entrance of houses using colors, flowers, and diyas. Families gather together to perform Lakshmi Puja in the evening and pray for happiness, health, and success. People also wear new clothes and prepare many traditional sweets and delicious foods to celebrate the festival with joy and excitement.

Diwali is also a time for sharing love and happiness with family and friends. People visit each other's homes and exchange sweets, gifts, and greetings. Children enjoy lighting sparklers and watching fireworks in the night sky. Markets and shops become very crowded as people buy decorations, clothes, and gifts. The entire country looks bright and festive during this time, creating a wonderful atmosphere of celebration and unity among people.
